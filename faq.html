FAQ

Questions I get asked often. If yours isn't here, send me a message.

What do you do?

I'm a software developer. I work on systems-level software: browser engines, Linux desktop platforms, mobile OS components, blockchain infrastructure, and AI compute platforms. I've been doing this for over a decade, mostly in open source.

What open source projects have you contributed to?

V8, WebKit, Mozilla SpiderMonkey and Gecko, GNOME (Telepathy), KDE Plasma (I built KBFX), and GStreamer, among others. You can see the full list on my projects page.

Are you available for hire?

Yes. I do consulting, architecture design, and hands-on development. Check the hire me page for details on engagement models and areas I cover.

What's CloudGPU?

CloudGPU is a platform I founded for AI compute and agent tooling. It provides GPU resources and infrastructure for building and deploying AI agents. Think of it as the plumbing that lets you run inference and agent workloads without managing the hardware yourself.

What's Hola Agent?

Hola Agent is a framework I built for creating autonomous AI agents. It handles tool use, memory, and task planning so you can build agents that actually do things rather than just chat.

What's efinder.lk?

It's a fact-checking engine that uses AI agents to verify claims and give you sourced fact-checks. Built for the Sri Lankan information landscape, where misinformation spreads fast and fact-checking resources are thin.

What's your background in blockchain?

I've worked on BTC and Bitcoin Core, LiquidBTC, and sidechain technology. Most of my work has been at the protocol and ledger level: consensus mechanisms, federated infrastructure, and decentralized application platforms.

Why is this website so old-school?

Because I like it this way. It loads fast, works in any browser, and doesn't need JavaScript. I hand-write the HTML in vi, just like I've always done. No frameworks, no build tools, no tracking. Just content.

Can I see your code?

Most of my open source work is on GitHub. Some of my older contributions are in the GNOME, KDE, Mozilla, and WebKit repositories. The links page has the full list.

How can I contact you?

Email is best. Check the contact page for my email address, PGP key, and other ways to reach me. I usually respond within a couple of days.

Do you mentor or take on interns?

Sometimes. If you're working on something in my area of expertise and you're stuck, reach out and I'll see if I can help. I don't have a formal program, but I'm happy to point people in the right direction or review code when I have time.

What editor do you use?

vi. Specifically Neovim these days, but I started with classic vi and I still use it when I need to. I've tried other editors over the years, but I always come back.
